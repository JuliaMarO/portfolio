Problem

MQTT, a network protocol often used by IoT devices, is notoriously insecure. Some MQTT devices are open to anonymous connections, which means that anyone can subscribe to messages sent to and from those devices or even publish messages themselves. I can sit at my desk and turn the lights on and off in a house across the country.

Research

My teammates, Li Kang Tan and Adam Taranissi, and I analyzed the personal information leakage and privacy risks of MQTT devices. Using the Shodan API, we surveyed the MQTT network for devices that permitted anonymous connections, then aggregated the responses to examine geographic trends and the scale of personal data leakage. We also examined a single open MQTT connection to verify that we could publish as well as subscribe to messages. Details of our methods and findings will be in our final report.

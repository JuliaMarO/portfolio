Problem

Decentralized VPNs (dVPNs) are a popular solution to security issues in the classical centralized VPN model. dVPNs use networks of volunteer node runners to distribute traffic, but being a node runnner is difficult: you can only lend bandwidth to one provider at once, and you have little control over the type of traffic routed through your machine.

Solution

The RING dVPN framework is a research project at Northwestern University that aims to create a centralized pool of node runners and to give those nodes control over when and how dVPN providers use their bandwidth. My project partner Jordan Zax and I made additions to RING that allow node runners to customize their network policies, set bandwidth and time limits to avoid network congestion, and enforce those decisions to dVPN providers.

Problem

Ming Yan, a provenance-based IDS project at Northwestern University, fails to flag certain attacks against a host machine executed from within a Docker container. One of the behaviors it fails to flag is running executable files from within a shared /tmp directory from inside the container: a permitted, but suspicious, action.

Solution

Under the mentorship of Yinhong Luo and Ling Jin, Braden Svoboda, Yuanjun Zhang, and I added code to the Ming Yan client that causes the IDS to flag this kind of behavior. However, our update created a rash of false positives. We finished our contributions to the project by working to diagnose and resolve false alarms while still identifying questionable /tmp behavior. We discuss our results in detail, along with next steps for resolution, in our final report.
